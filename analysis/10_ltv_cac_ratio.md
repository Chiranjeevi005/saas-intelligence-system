# LTV to CAC Ratio Analysis

## Key Metrics

- **Average LTV**: 2,279.58
- **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)**: 650
- **LTV:CAC Ratio**: 3.51

---

## Interpretation

- The business is operating under positive and healthy unit economics.
- Each customer generates approximately 3.5x more revenue than the cost required to acquire them.
- An LTV:CAC ratio of >3 is generally considered a benchmark for a successful and scalable SaaS business.

---

## Business Insight

> The current growth model is financially sustainable and ready for scaling.

- The primary challenge is not the cost of acquisition, but the high churn in specific premium segments (Fiber optic).
- Improving churn in the high-risk segments would push this ratio even higher (potentially towards 4:1 or 5:1).

---

## Root Cause Analysis

- Long-term contracts and loyal customers are subsidizing the high acquisition cost.
- High churn in the Month-to-month segment is the main drag on the potential LTV.

---

## Scenario Analysis

If churn in the high-risk segment (currently 60%) is reduced:

- Customer lifetime for that cohort increases.
- Average LTV increases significantly above 2,279.
- LTV:CAC ratio approaches world-class levels (4.0+).

---

## Strategic Recommendation

### 1. Accelerate Acquisition
- Given the healthy 3.51 ratio, the business can afford to invest more in acquisition while maintaining profitability.

### 2. Transition High-Risk Users
- Convert high-churn month-to-month users to 1-year plans to secure their LTV early.

### 3. Optimize Channel Spend
- Double down on acquisition channels that align with 1-year or 2-year contract behaviors.

---

## Impact

- Moves business toward aggressive but stable growth.
- Improves profitability without increasing the relative marketing spend.

---

## Final Takeaway

> With a healthy LTV:CAC ratio of 3.51, the business is in a strong position to scale, provided it addresses the structural churn "leaks" in the premium Fiber segment.

# Customer Lifetime Value (LTV) Estimation

## Objective
Estimate the revenue generated by each customer over their lifetime using tenure and monthly charges.

---

## Key Findings
- Customer lifetime value varies significantly across users based on tenure and subscription pricing.
- Users with shorter tenure contribute disproportionately lower revenue to the business.

---

## Interpretation
- Customers who churn early generate minimal value, making it difficult to recover acquisition costs.
- High churn segments (identified earlier) directly correlate with low lifetime value.

---

## Business Insight
> Churn is the primary driver of reduced customer lifetime value.

- Month-to-month users and high-risk segments (fiber + electronic check) likely fall into low-LTV categories.
- Long-term customers contribute significantly higher revenue due to extended tenure.

---

## Recommendation
- Focus on increasing customer lifetime (tenure) rather than only increasing acquisition.
- Prioritize retention strategies for high-risk users to improve LTV distribution.

---

## Impact
- Increasing average tenure directly increases LTV without increasing acquisition cost.
- Improves overall profitability per customer.
